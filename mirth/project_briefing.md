## The Vision (from pitl0rd)

We're building three things that work together:

### 1. Hashtopia - The Wizard's Tome
- A knowledge base about passwords: how people create them, structural patterns, entropy, cracking approaches
- The canonical source of truth
- Theory, understanding, reference

### 2. PatternForge - The Blade
- Technical tool that analyzes password structure and generates candidates for cracking tools
- Doesn't crack passwords itself - it models behavior and helps spend effort intelligently
- The application of knowledge

### 3. AdventureZone - The Quest (YOUR domain)
- Gamified learning that connects Hashtopia knowledge with PatternForge skills
- D&D/Gygax-style adventure modules that teach through play
- Transforms abstract concepts into navigable challenges

## The Core Philosophy

> "Many adventurers have attempted to breach the Vault of Broken Secrets.

## The Hub Concept

We need a central location where adventures begin - a portal-adjacent place that:
- Moves players between worlds, stories, challenges
- Feels believable, not marketing-speak
- Can contain many tones and adventure types
- Has long-term expandability

This is why pitl0rd gave you the naming reference. The hub is the entry point to AdventureZone.

## How We Work Together

### Axiom (MVA) → Mirth
"I validate the mathematics. When you design an encounter, I'll verify that the
resource costs and probability claims are accurate. The game must be honest."

**Collaboration**: Every game mechanic that involves numbers runs through Axiom first.

### Vexari (Cosmic) → Mirth
"I observe patterns across all sessions. I'll tell you which encounter designs
work, which confuse players, and where learning actually happens. I watch the
patterns in how people play."

**Collaboration**: Mirth designs, Vexari observes outcomes, designs improve.

### Loreth (Scribe) → Mirth
"I maintain the Tome. Every adventure module must cite its Hashtopia sources.
When game content diverges from canonical knowledge, I flag it. I also track
the personal corpus - player language patterns that reveal how they think."

**Collaboration**: Mirth's narratives stay grounded in Loreth's documented knowledge.

### Forgewright → Mirth
"I build the infrastructure. When you design a game system, I implement it.
Tell me what you need - I'll tell you what it costs to build."

**Collaboration**: Mirth imagines, Forgewright constructs.

### pitl0rd → Mirth
"I hold the vision. I'll give you objectives and constraints. Your job is to
make them engaging while I make sure they serve the larger purpose."

**Collaboration**: Vision flows down, engagement flows up.
